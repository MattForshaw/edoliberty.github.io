Bio

I am now a Principal Scientist and the manager of the Amazon AI Algorithms group. I am looking to hire great engineers and scientists to tackle some of the most interesting problems in machine learning, data science, and scalable systems. Please reach out to me at libertye@amazon.com with any questions on openings. The following openings indicate New York as their location but apply also for Seattle WA and Palo Alto CA.

    • Senior Software Development Engineer - Amazon AI Algorithms
  • Software Development Manager - Amazon AI Algorithms
There are more openings in all our locations (not just New York)

Up until recently I was the head of Yahoo's Independent Research Group in New York where we focused on scalable machine learning and data mining for Yahoo critical applications.

I received my B.Sc in Physics and Computer Science from Tel Aviv university and my Ph.D in Computer Science from Yale University, under the supervision of Steven Zucker. After that, I was a Post-Doctoral fellow at Yale in Program in Applied Mathematics.

My personal research interests include fast dimensionality reduction, clustering, streaming and online algorithms, machine learning, and large scale numerical linear algebra. I am especially fond of randomized algorithms and high dimensional geometry.

Some Code

Data Sketches: I'm glad to announce that Yahoo open sourced parts of our data sketches library. This is an ongoing effort which I am proud to be a part of. There are many exciting new algorithms coming in later releases.

Frequent Directions: I have been asked to make some matrix sketching code available for a long time now. So, Mina Ghashami and I made some of our frequent direction git repo public. This code is distributed freely for academic use only. Please feel free to send pull requests.

Streaming Quantiles in Python: I'm excited about resolving one of the longests standing open problems in the streaming model. We designed an optimal algorithm for finding any approximate quantile of a stream of elements. See also the paper which Zohar Karnin, Kevin Lang, and myself posted on Arxiv.
